I am a neuroscientist studying how organisms adapt to changing environments through experience-dependent plasticity. My work spans multiple levels of biological organization, from behavioral adaptation and neural circuit dynamics to structural remodeling of sensory tissues. Using approaches that combine physiology, quantitative imaging, and computational analysis, I investigate how environmental experience reshapes sensory systems and the cellular structures that support them.

My research has explored experience-dependent modulation of top-down sensory processing in cortical circuits, plasticity of peripheral sensory neurons in Drosophila models of nociception, and structural remodeling of retina and retinal pigment epithelium in mammalian blindness model systems. Together these studies reflect a broader interest in how biological systems maintain flexibility in the face of environmental challenge.

I am particularly interested in integrating physiological and structural approaches with molecular analyses to understand how adaptive plasticity is stabilized and regulated at the level of gene expression and cellular pathways.
