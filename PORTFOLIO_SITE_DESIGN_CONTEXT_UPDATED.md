# Scientific Portfolio Website -- Design and Strategy Context (Updated)

Author: Kevin J. Donaldson Platform: GitHub Pages + Academic Pages
(Minimal Mistakes) Purpose: Long‑term professional research portfolio

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. Purpose of the Website

This site serves as a centralized, curated professional portfolio for:

-   Postdoctoral applications
-   Academic research positions
-   Industry R&D roles
-   Collaborators and funding partners

The website functions as:

-   A conceptual map of research trajectory
-   A demonstration of methodological depth
-   A signal of intellectual direction
-   A stable public-facing academic identity

The site is intentionally **not**:

-   A blog
-   A personal branding site
-   A duplicate of the CV
-   A promotional or marketing document

Instead, it is designed to communicate scientific direction and
intellectual coherence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:

-   Postdoctoral principal investigators
-   Systems neuroscience laboratories
-   Plasticity researchers
-   Molecular neuroscience laboratories studying environmental
    regulation of gene expression

Secondary audience:

-   Industry R&D scientists
-   Academic hiring committees
-   Collaborators

Audience expectations:

-   Conceptual clarity
-   Technical competence
-   Research trajectory
-   Intellectual maturity

Tone should signal:

-   Scientific rigor
-   Restraint
-   Depth
-   Forward-looking direction

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Theme Choice

The **Academic Pages** template was selected because:

-   Clean and professional design
-   Widely used in academic communities
-   Minimal visual noise
-   Flexible content structure
-   Strong support for research and publication pages

The theme derives from **Minimal Mistakes**, which emphasizes readable
typography and structured content.

Design philosophy:

> Understated credibility over visual novelty.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Site Architecture

Navigation structure:

Home\
Research\
Publications\
Teaching\
Outreach\
Methods & Skills\
Gallery\
CV

The order reflects the desired narrative:

1.  Scientific identity
2.  Research content
3.  Evidence of work
4.  Supporting materials
5.  Credentials

Gallery appears before CV so that visitors encounter **scientific output
and visual evidence of research** before formal documentation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. Research Page Structure

The Research page is the conceptual core of the site.

Structure:

Overview

Research Areas

Experience‑Dependent Modulation of Sensory Perception

Experience‑Dependent Modulation of Sensory Neurons

Structural Remodeling of Sensory Tissue

Future Directions

This structure reflects plasticity operating at multiple levels of
biological organization:

1.  Cortical modulation of perception
2.  Experience-dependent plasticity in sensory neurons
3.  Tissue-level remodeling

The **Overview** provides the conceptual umbrella linking these areas
through environmental experience and adaptive plasticity.

The **Future Directions** section signals a trajectory toward:

-   molecular regulation
-   gene expression
-   regulatory mechanisms underlying plasticity

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Methods & Skills Page

The Methods & Skills page demonstrates technical depth beyond
publications.

Expected categories:

Experimental methods - electrophysiology - calcium imaging - confocal
microscopy - behavioral assays

Quantitative analysis - image analysis pipelines - morphometric
analysis - behavioral quantification

Computational tools - MATLAB - R - Python - custom analysis software
(e.g., ETHOS)

The purpose is to signal methodological capability to both academic and
industry audiences.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 7. Gallery Page Philosophy

The Gallery page provides **visual evidence of scientific work**.

Content types may include:

Microscopy - confocal imaging - retinal flatmount mosaics -
immunostaining

Quantitative imaging - segmentation overlays - morphometric heatmaps -
radial bin analyses

Behavior - larval behavioral imaging - pose estimation examples

Figures - selected figures from publications

The gallery is intended to make the site feel like the portfolio of an
**active experimental scientist**, rather than a static document
repository.

# 9. Strategic Scientific Positioning

The site communicates a trajectory toward research integrating:

-   environmental adaptation
-   neural plasticity
-   structural remodeling
-   molecular regulation

This progression moves naturally from systems neuroscience toward
molecular neuroscience.

The positioning aligns with laboratories studying:

-   environmental regulation of gene expression
-   molecular mechanisms of plasticity
-   systems-to-molecular integration

This alignment is intentionally subtle and embedded within the research
narrative rather than explicitly stated.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 10. Writing Style Constraints

Avoid:

-   buzzwords
-   exaggerated claims
-   defensive language
-   overly promotional tone

Prefer:

-   conceptual clarity
-   precise language
-   restrained claims
-   forward-looking direction

Sections should typically conclude by:

-   synthesizing contribution
-   reinforcing conceptual themes
-   indicating future research direction
