# Scientific Portfolio Website – Design and Strategy Context

Author: Kevin J. Donaldson  
Platform: GitHub Pages + Academic Pages (Minimal Mistakes)  
Purpose: Long-term professional research portfolio  

---

# 1. Why This Website Exists

This site serves as a centralized, curated professional portfolio for:

- Postdoctoral applications
- Academic research positions
- Industry R&D roles
- Collaborators and funding partners

It is intended to function as:
- A conceptual map of research trajectory
- A demonstration of methodological depth
- A signal of intellectual direction
- A stable public-facing academic identity

It is NOT:
- A blog
- A flashy personal brand site
- A CV duplicate
- A buzzword-heavy narrative

---

# 2. Target Audience

Primary:
- Postdoctoral PIs (systems neuroscience, plasticity, molecular neuroscience, cognition)
- Labs studying environmental adaptation and gene regulation
- Investigators integrating physiology with molecular tools

Secondary:
- Industry scientists (biotech, neurotechnology, data science)
- Academic hiring committees

Audience expectations:
- Conceptual clarity
- Intellectual maturity
- Technical competence
- Trajectory toward molecular integration

Tone must signal:
- Scientific rigor
- Restraint
- Depth
- Forward-looking direction

---

# 3. Why Academic Pages (Theme Choice)

Academic Pages was selected because:

- Clean, minimal, professional design
- Widely used in academic communities
- Low visual noise
- Easy navigation
- Supports structured research sections
- Works well for future expansion (gallery, methods, publications)

We intentionally avoided:
- Highly stylized or startup-like themes
- Overly minimalist one-page sites
- Blog-forward templates

Design goal:
Understated credibility over visual novelty.

---

# 4. Structural Philosophy

The site is structured bottom-up.

We began with the Research page rather than the Home page because:

- Identity emerges from substance.
- The author defines himself through work, not slogans.
- Conceptual coherence matters more than branding.
- The Home page will be distilled from Research once stable.

Research page structure:

1. Overview (conceptual umbrella)
2. Physiological and Behavioral Plasticity in Sensory Systems
3. Structural and Tissue-Level Plasticity (Retina/RPE)
4. Future molecular integration trajectory

Teaching and Outreach will receive equal structural weight later.

---

# 5. Conceptual Identity

Central theme:

How organisms respond to changing environments through multiscale plasticity.

Modes of response:
- Behavioral adaptation
- Sensory processing modulation
- Tissue and structural remodeling
- Genetic and molecular regulation (future direction)

Plasticity is framed as:
- Context-dependent
- Environment-driven
- Multilevel
- Constrained as well as enabling

This is NOT framed as:
- Purely neural signal analysis
- Purely disease research
- Purely molecular biology
- A chronological CV narrative

---

# 6. Writing Constraints and Style Rules

Avoid:
- Triadic lists
- Buzzwords
- Overclaiming
- Defensive phrasing
- Explicit statements of what was not achieved
- Overemphasis on any single institutional phase

Prefer:
- Conceptual coherence
- Accurate authorship framing
- Balanced emphasis
- Precise but restrained language
- Forward-looking but not speculative claims

End sections by:
- Synthesizing contribution
- Signaling trajectory
- Reinforcing conceptual unity

---

# 7. Branching Workflow

master → live production site  
local-dev → development sandbox  

Rules:
- Never develop directly on master.
- Commit frequently on local-dev.
- Push local-dev for cloud backup.
- Merge into master only when ready to publish.

# 9. Overall Presentation Philosophy

The site should convey:

A scientist who:
- Thinks mechanistically
- Works across scales
- Values rigor
- Is transitioning toward molecular integration
- Has established systems that justify deeper inquiry

The site should feel:
- Deliberate
- Cohesive
- Mature
- Scientifically grounded

It should not feel:
- Promotional
- Fragmented
- Trend-driven
- Overdesigned
